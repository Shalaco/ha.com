10 Facts About Hearing Loss

While there are many facts about hearing loss, these ten demonstrate just how prevalent hearing loss is, and how it affects an individual.

Download PDF Print Checklist
3 out of every 1,000 children in the U.S. are born deaf or hard-of-hearing. 
9 out of every 10 children who are born deaf are born to parents who can hear. 
Approximately 4,000 new cases of sudden deafness occur each year in the U.S.
9 out of every 10 people who experience sudden deafness only have it in one ear.
Approximately 36 million American adults report some degree of hearing loss. 
18% of Baby-Boomers (ages 48-64) have hearing loss.
29% of people over age 65 have hearing loss. 
Men are more likely to experience hearing loss than women. 
Those with age-related hearing loss usually struggle to hear high-pitched, versus low-pitched sounds.  
Only 1 out of 5 people who could benefit from a hearing aid actually wears one.
